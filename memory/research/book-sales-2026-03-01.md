## Executive Summary

The behavioral psychology/decision-making book market on Amazon remains highly competitive in 2026, dominated by perennial classics like Kahneman's *Thinking, Fast and Slow* and Cialdini's *Influence*. However, emerging algorithm changes (A10 rollout), evolving reader search behavior, and underserved sub-niches present strategic opportunities for *The Divided Brain* to gain visibility and market share.

**Key Finding:** The 2026 Amazon A10 algorithm now heavily weights engagement signals (click-through rate, time on page, conversion) over pure sales velocity, creating an opening for well-optimized mid-list titles to compete with established bestsellers.

## 1. CATEGORY OPPORTUNITIES

### Current Amazon Category Landscape

Amazon's 2026 category system now limits new books to **3 browse categories** (down from the previous 10-category system). Category selection has become more critical than ever.

| Category | Competition Level | #1 BSR Threshold | Strategic Value |

| **Science & Math > Biological Sciences > Neuroscience** | Low-Medium | ~10,000-20,000 overall | **Untapped opportunity** |

### Strategic Category Recommendations

**Better Placement Than Current (if applicable):**

1. **Add Neuroscience angle** - The "Neuroscience" category has significantly lower competition (BSR thresholds 2-4x higher) and growing reader interest. Reframe positioning to emphasize the brain science/hemisphere research foundation.

2. **Business Decision-Making subcategory** - Often overlooked by pure psychology books. Readers here have purchase intent and price tolerance ($15-25 range).

3. **Consumer Behavior** - Under the Business & Money umbrella, this niche has fewer competing titles than general behavioral psychology while capturing marketing/business readers.

### 2026 Category Tactics

- **Use BISAC codes strategically**: The 3-category limit applies to browse categories, but correct BISAC codes can get you into additional search indexes
- **Monitor category BSR daily**: Tools like Publisher Rocket or BookBeam track which categories have the lowest #1 bestseller threshold
- **Request category changes post-launch**: Contact KDP support with sales momentum to request additional categories after 30+ days of sales data

## 4. AMAZON ALGORITHM UPDATES (2025-2026)

### The A10 Algorithm Rollout

Amazon quietly replaced the A9 algorithm with **A10** starting in May 2025, with full deployment by late 2025. This represents the most significant change to book discoverability in years.

### Key A10 Changes Affecting Non-Fiction Books

#### 1. **Engagement Signals Now Weighted Heavier**
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** from search results: +40% weight increase
- **Time on Page**: Readers spending >60 seconds signals quality
- **Conversion Rate**: Visitors who purchase vs. bounce
- **Implication:** Your cover, title, and first 100 words of description are critical

#### 2. **Metadata Quality Over Quantity**
- Book title keywords matter more than ever
- Subtitle optimization crucial for indexing
- Series consistency recognized and rewarded
- **Implication:** Front-load keywords in subtitle; maintain consistent branding

#### 3. **Customer Satisfaction Metrics**
- Review velocity (new reviews weighted more than total count)
- Return rates (lower = better ranking)
- Kindle Unlimited read-through rates
- **Implication:** Drive early reviews aggressively; ensure quality to minimize returns

#### 4. **Prime and FBA Preference Reduced for Books**
- Unlike physical products, Prime eligibility now has **neutral** weight for books
- Print-on-demand no longer penalized vs. warehouse stock
- **Implication:** KDP Print is now competitive with traditional publishing for ranking

#### 5. **Amazon Advertising Integration**
- Sponsored Products ads now influence organic rank indirectly through sales velocity
- Amazon Attribution tracking improved for external traffic
- **Implication:** Ad spend can "prime the pump" for organic visibility

### A10 Optimization Checklist for *The Divided Brain*

- [ ] **Cover design**: Professional, readable at thumbnail size (test in search results)
- [ ] **Title testing**: A/B test subtitle variations using Amazon Ads
- [ ] **Description optimization**: Hook in first 100 characters; include keywords naturally
- [ ] **Review strategy**: Target 25 reviews in first 30 days
- [ ] **Look Inside**: Optimize first 10 pages for engagement (TOC, compelling intro)
- [ ] **Series page**: If applicable, create series for enhanced visibility

## 6. ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS

### RECOMMENDATION #1: Implement Aggressive Review Generation Campaign

**The Problem:** A10 algorithm heavily weights review velocity. *The Divided Brain* needs 25+ reviews in first 30 days to trigger algorithmic visibility.

**Implementation:**
1. **Pre-launch ARC team:** Recruit 50-100 behavioral psychology enthusiasts, business book bloggers, LinkedIn followers
   - Source from: Paul's LinkedIn network, relevant subreddits (r/behavioraleconomics, r/decisionmaking), Goodreads groups
   - Deliver ARCs 3-4 weeks before launch
   - Follow up twice (1 week before, day of launch)

2. **Launch week promotion:**
   - Price at $0.99 for first 5 days (maximize unit velocity)
   - Email list blast (if available)
   - LinkedIn announcement with direct Amazon link
   - Ask ARC readers to post reviews within 48 hours of launch

3. **Review monitoring:**
   - Use tool like BookReport or ReaderScout to track new reviews
   - Respond professionally to all reviews (especially critical ones)
   - Report any policy violations promptly

**Expected Outcome:** 30-50 reviews in first month, triggering A10 algorithm boost for organic visibility.
