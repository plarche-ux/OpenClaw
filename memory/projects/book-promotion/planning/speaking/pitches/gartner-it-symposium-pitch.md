**The Divided Brain Framework**

My work in behavioral psychology reveals a split that explains why brilliant IT strategy dies in presentation rooms. The Old Brain (the ancient, emotional decision-maker) evaluates every proposal through five filters before logic ever engages: Threat Detection, Return on Effort, Status Signaling, Contrast and Comparison, and Visual Override. The New Brain (the rational cortex) then builds the business case to justify what the Old Brain has already decided.

IT leaders typically speak to the New Brain. They lead with architecture diagrams, ROI calculations, and risk matrices. Meanwhile, the Old Brain of their audience has already categorized the message as threat, burden, or ego challenge. The decision is made before the slide deck advances.

This framework explains why digital transformations stall, why shadow IT proliferates, and why the best technical solutions lose to vendors who understand human decision-making.
