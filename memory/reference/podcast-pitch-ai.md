# Podcast Pitch: AI and the Divided Brain

## Overview
**Subject:** Paul Larche – Canadian media entrepreneur and behavioral science expert.
**Book:** THE DIVIDED BRAIN: Understanding Human Behaviour in Branding and Business.

## The Hook
How AI taps into our “Old Brain” instincts, driving what we click, buy, and believe subconsciously by targeting emotional triggers and eroding trust.

## Core Message
Paul speaks to practical ways leaders and consumers can stay grounded in reflection when systems are built for speed and reaction. The “Old Brain” seeks safety, status, and belonging and reacts faster than reason. The “New Brain” is slower and more reflective, yet modern platforms rarely engage it. Paul calls metacognition the “clutch” that helps people notice impulses and choose responses.

## Key Talking Points
- **Press Secretary vs. CEO:** How emotion leads and logic follows.
- **Trust as Currency:** Why trust is the defining measure of credibility in an AI-shaped environment.
- **Vulnerability:** Where we are most susceptible to subtle emotional influence online.
- **The "Clutch":** Simple mental habits to slow down reactions and exit autopilot.

## Suggested Interview Questions
1. Why has trust become such a defining measure of credibility in an AI-shaped environment?
2. Where are people most vulnerable to subtle emotional influence online?
3. What changes when personalization makes technology feel “personal” to the user?
4. In plain language, what do you mean by the Old Brain and the New Brain?
5. Why do emotions so often outrun logic when people interact with technology?
6. How do you see the “press secretary vs. CEO” dynamic show up in everyday decisions?
7. What are the warning signs that someone is on autopilot rather than thinking clearly?
8. What simple mental habits help people “press in the clutch” and slow a reaction down?
9. How can leaders communicate in a way that persuades without exploiting emotion?
10. What guardrails can teams use to test whether messaging builds trust or just extracts attention?
11. For listeners who want to go deeper, what does THE DIVIDED BRAIN offer that’s most immediately useful?
12. How can organizations apply your Old Brain/New Brain framework inside branding, marketing, and internal communications?
